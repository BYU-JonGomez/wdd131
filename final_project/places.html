Flowers Watch

The Flower Clock, inaugurated in 1962 as part of the FIFA World Cup hosted by Chile, quickly became a symbol of Viña del Mar. Strategically located at the entrance of the city near Caleta Abarca Beach, it welcomes thousands of visitors every year. Tourists from all over the world flock to this site to take pictures with the beautifully arranged flowers, which change seasonally.
This constant influx of visitors directly benefits the local economy by increasing demand for hotels, restaurants, and transportation services. Many tourists extend their stay in Viña del Mar to explore other attractions like the Quinta Vergara, the famous casino, and the coastline, leading to greater revenue for the hospitality industry.

Reñaca Beach

Reñaca Beach, one of Viña del Mar's most famous coastal attractions, is a hotspot for tourists, surfers, and sunbathers. Known for its golden sands and powerful waves, it attracts visitors from Chile and beyond, especially during the summer. Viña del Mar, founded in 1878, grew as a seaside resort for the elite, later becoming a major tourist and cultural hub. The city blends historical elegance with modern infrastructure, boasting iconic sites like the Flower Clock and the Quinta Vergara Palace. Reñaca has evolved into a vibrant district with luxury apartments, restaurants, and nightlife, solidifying its status as a must-visit destination in Chile.

Quinta Vergara

Quinta Vergara is one of Viña del Mar's most iconic landmarks, known for its beautiful gardens, historic palace, and world-famous music festival. Originally the estate of the Vergara family, it features the stunning Vergara Palace, a National Monument showcasing neo-Gothic and Venetian influences. The park surrounding the palace is filled with exotic plants, sculptures, and shaded pathways, making it a favorite spot for visitors. Quinta Vergara is best known for hosting the annual Viña del Mar International Song Festival, attracting global artists and thousands of spectators. This cultural and historical gem remains a symbol of Viña del Mar's artistic and natural beauty, drawing tourists year-round.
